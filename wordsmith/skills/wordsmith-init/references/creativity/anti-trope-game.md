### 8.2 Combat Flow Pattern
1. Thấy địch → Check thẻ trạng thái (Giám định)
2. Đánh giá sức mạnh
3. Chiến thuật
4. Hành động
5. Kết quả + loot/exp

### 8.3 Underdog-to-Stronger Patterns
1. Yếu nhất, bị khinh thường
2. Tìm ra skill độc đáo
3. Rèn luyện chăm chỉ
4. Chiến thắng bất ngờ
5. Được công nhận

### 8.4 Training Pacing
- Level up khi đủ điểm kinh nghiệm
- Cần săn quái vật để tăng cấp
- 5% chance hồi phục khi level up

### 8.5 Inner Monologue Patterns
- Unreliable narrator: 'Ta thật vô dụng' - nhưng thực tế khác
- Inner monologue trong ngoặc đơn đơn: 'Ừm, có vẻ ngon nghẻ đấy!'
- Self-deprecating humor với nhận xét mỉa mai

## I. Common Tropes (to avoid or subvert)

| Trope | Problem | Frequency |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Homogenized system flow (unlimited cheat) | Market saturation, lack of differentiation | Extremely high |
| Uncapped stats (loss of anticipation) | Readers lose motivation to follow | Extremely high |
| Still gaining cheats in Ch.2 (dragging pace) | Slow early pacing, high drop rate | High |
| No clear opponent by Ch.3 | Lack of conflict, reader attrition | High |
| Over-explaining worldbuilding in first 5 chapters | Fatally boring, readers skip | High |
| No clear short-term goal for protagonist | Readers don't know why to keep reading | Medium |
| IP fusion with no new logic (pure copy-paste) | Lacks creativity, no surprise for readers | Medium |

## II. Anti-Convention Constraints (select at least 1 per project)

### 2.1 System/Cheat Constraints

| ID | Anti-Trope Rule | Creative Driver | Example |
|----|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| GM-001 | Cheat has a clear cap (time/uses/energy cost) | Resource management becomes core strategy | Trial card only lasts 5 minutes, 24-hour cooldown after use |
| GM-002 | Cheat activation requires conditions (not always available) | Creating the conditions becomes the plot core | System only activates during extreme danger |
| GM-003 | Cheat has random negative side effects | Risk and reward coexist | 20% chance of triggering random penalty on each use |
| GM-004 | Others discovering the cheat changes the rules | Hiding and protecting become core tension | After system is discovered, all players target the protagonist |
| GM-005 | Cheat conflicts with game/world rules | Rule conflicts create sustained tension | System-granted skills violate the game's physics |

### 2.2 Growth Constraints

| ID | Anti-Trope Rule | Creative Driver | Example |
|----|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| GM-006 | Stats have a ceiling (requires breakthrough mechanism) | Breakthrough becomes a staged climax | Level cap 100, breakthrough requires completing special quests |
| GM-007 | Leveling requires real cost (not just grinding) | Every growth has weight | Leveling up requires abandoning an existing skill |
| GM-008 | Powerful abilities have side effects | The stronger the power, the higher the cost | After using the strongest skill, pass out for three days |
| GM-009 | Resources are scarce (can't be obtained infinitely) | Resource competition becomes core conflict | Only 10 rare material respawn points across the entire server |
| GM-010 | Higher levels bring bigger threats (non-linear safety) | Getting stronger doesn't mean safer | After breaking through to S-rank, S-rank monsters start actively hunting you |

### 2.3 Combat/Competition Constraints

| ID | Anti-Trope Rule | Creative Driver | Example |
|----|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| GM-011 | Can't challenge above rank without limit | Must find a strategic breakthrough | Challenging above rank triggers a system penalty mechanism |
| GM-012 | Victory has a cost (consumption/exposure/debt) | Victory is not free | Won the match but exposed core tactics |
| GM-013 | Rules can be exploited by opponents | Rules are double-edged swords | Loophole found by protagonist is used in reverse by opponents |
| GM-014 | Inheriting the previous person's enemies | Growth brings new trouble | After defeating someone strong, all their enemies transfer to the protagonist |
| GM-015 | Some rules cannot be broken (game mechanics/physics) | Constraint boundaries create realism | No matter how strong, can't break the game's basic physics |

### 2.4 World/Information Constraints

| ID | Anti-Trope Rule | Creative Driver | Example |
|----|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| GM-016 | Protagonist doesn't know the complete rules (information asymmetry) | Exploring rules becomes sustained motivation | Manual only has half the pages, the other half must be discovered |
| GM-017 | Rules evolve over time | Protagonist must continuously adapt | Game rules update weekly, previous strategies become invalid |
| GM-018 | Different players/factions interpret rules differently | Information gaps create strategic gameplay | Protagonist discovers a rule interpretation others don't know |
| GM-019 | Forgetting mechanism (can't accumulate information infinitely) | Information management becomes core | Each death causes partial memory loss |
| GM-020 | Revealing/displaying abilities changes the rules | Hiding becomes a survival strategy | After publicly using a skill, the system weakens it |

## III. Non-Trope Cool Points (replacing traditional face-slapping)

### 3.1 Strategy-Type Cool Points

| ID | Cool Point Pattern | Description | Example |
|----|--------------------|-------------|---------|
| GM-S01 | Rule Exploitation | Using rule loopholes against opponents | Using game judgment mechanics to make opponents self-destruct |
| GM-S02 | Behavior Prediction | Predicting AI/NPC/opponent behavior patterns | Analyzing opponent's behavior patterns and planning three steps ahead |
| GM-S03 | Cross-System Transfer | Migrating knowledge across systems/games | Using real-world physics knowledge to solve in-game puzzles |
| GM-S04 | Weak Beats Strong | Defeating superior resources with inferior ones | Using the lowest-grade materials to craft the highest-grade equipment |
| GM-S05 | Hidden Mechanics | Discovering and exploiting hidden mechanics | Finding a hidden dungeon that no one in the game knows about |

### 3.2 Cost-Type Cool Points

| ID | Cool Point Pattern | Description | Example |
|----|--------------------|-------------|---------|
| GM-S06 | Costly Victory | Victory that requires paying a real price | Exhausting all resources for one critical victory |
| GM-S07 | Side Effect Weaponization | Using an ability's side effects as a weapon | Using a skill's negative effects to damage the opponent |
| GM-S08 | Optimal Under Constraints | Finding the optimal solution within limitations | Defeating a BOSS with only basic equipment |
| GM-S09 | Short-term Sacrifice | Sacrificing short-term benefits for long-term advantage | Voluntarily losing a ranking to gain a better resource position |
| GM-S10 | Fighting With Their Own Rules | Defeating enemies using their own rules | Using the opponent's designed trap to turn the tables |

### 3.3 Reversal-Type Cool Points

| ID | Cool Point Pattern | Description | Example |
|----|--------------------|-------------|---------|
| GM-S11 | Bug Becomes Core | An ability considered a bug becomes core combat power | A useless skill everyone mocked becomes the ultimate weapon |
| GM-S12 | Failure as Optimal Solution | A seemingly losing choice revealed to be the best | Protagonist chose the worst class but discovers hidden advantages |
| GM-S13 | Rule Subversion | The rules themselves are overturned | Protagonist discovers the underlying logic of the entire game is wrong |
| GM-S14 | NPC Awakening | NPC/AI displays unexpected complexity | An NPC thought to be a tool turns out to have their own agenda |
| GM-S15 | Truth Revealed | The truth of the game/world is revealed | Protagonist discovers the game world is a mirror of the real world |

## IV. Combination Examples

### Example 1: Game Fiction - IP Fusion

```
Anti-trope rules: GM-005 (cheat conflicts with game rules) + GM-016 (doesn't know complete rules)
Non-trope cool points: GM-S01 (rule exploitation) + GM-S12 (failure as optimal solution)

Core conflict: Transported into an IP world, but the original plot has already deviated, making the protagonist's "spoilers" increasingly inaccurate
Creative driver: How to find new survival strategies in a constantly changing plot?
Differentiation: Not "I know the plot so I'm invincible," but "what I know about the plot is increasingly wrong"
```

### Example 2: Apocalyptic Survival - Building

```
Anti-trope rules: GM-009 (resource scarcity) + GM-010 (higher level brings bigger threats)
Non-trope cool points: GM-S04 (weak beats strong) + GM-S09 (short-term sacrifice)

Core conflict: Crafting/building system vs. resource competition with other survivors; getting stronger attracts stronger threats
Creative driver: How to establish a sustainable survival system in a resource-scarce apocalypse?
Differentiation: Not "I have a system so I'm invincible," but "my system makes me everyone's target"
```

### Example 3: Sci-Fi - Cthulhu Hardcore

```
Anti-trope rules: GM-016 (doesn't know complete rules) + GM-017 (rules evolve)
Non-trope cool points: GM-S05 (hidden mechanics) + GM-S15 (truth revealed)

Core conflict: Rational protagonist searching for patterns in an irrational world, but the patterns themselves are constantly changing
Creative driver: How to maintain rationality in a world where rules constantly evolve?
Differentiation: Not "I found the pattern so I'm invincible," but "the pattern itself is a trap"
```

## V. Usage Guide

### 5.1 Selection Process

1. Select 1-2 items from "Anti-Convention Constraints"
2. Select 2-3 types from "Non-Trope Cool Points" as main cool point patterns
3. Check if the combination produces a "chemical reaction"
4. Design 2-3 unique plot solutions based on the constraints

### 5.2 Game/Sci-Fi Genre Special Notes

- ✅ **Cheat must appear in Chapter 1**: Cannot be delayed to Chapter 2
- ✅ **Clear opponent must appear by Chapter 3**: Environment/rules/specific villain, pick one
- ✅ **Stat feedback must be visualized**: Before/after comparison (combat power 50 → combat power 180)
- ✅ **Cheat must have a cap**: Trial cards have time limits, abilities have costs
- ✅ **At least 1 small cool point per chapter**: Can't go more than 1 chapter without a satisfaction beat
- ❌ System dialogue exceeding 1000 words (readers will skip it)
- ❌ Over-explaining worldbuilding in the first 5 chapters
- ❌ Protagonist's ability has no ceiling/cost

### 5.3 Avoid These Pitfalls

- ❌ Choosing a constraint then using a cheat to bypass it
- ❌ Constraints only valid early on and disappearing later
- ❌ Constraints conflict with cool point patterns
- ✅ Constraints run through the entire story
- ✅ Constraints drive plot development
- ✅ Constraints and cool points complement each other

## Vietnamese Writing Patterns (STYLE_GUIDE_VN.md Section 13)

### From Ta Dung Nhin Vo Lam Toang (Primary Source):
- Dialogue: "Nội dung" + action tag, no ——
- Inner thoughts: third-person narrative, "cậu" for self-reference
- Scene breaks: --- for major, *— Hết Chương —* for end
- First-person: cậu/mình in thoughts, tao/mày with close people
- Slang: vãi, cứt, bro (@ v @) in GenZ contexts

### Sentence Structure:
- Must have Subject + Predicate
- Connectors: và, nhưng, nên, vì, sau đó, rồi, thì, mà
- No fragmented sentences

### 8 Error Types:
1. Units: mét/cm/km/kg (NOT trượng/dặm/tấc/thốn/ly)
2. Vocabulary: match context
3. Punctuation: — single (NOT —— double)
4. Sentence: complete with subject+predicate
5. Spelling: ken két not kẽ kẽy
6. Connectors: required
7. Subject: explicit in descriptions
8. Natural Vietnamese words

### Cool-Point Placement:
- Every 500-800 words
- Fast-paced: 70-80% short sentences (1-7 words)
- Slow-paced: 70-80% long sentences (15+ words)

### Cliffhanger Patterns:
- Incomplete action ending
- System notification: "Keng!" + message
- Twist revelation
- Question left unanswered
