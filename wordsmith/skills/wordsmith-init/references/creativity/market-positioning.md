| Attribute | Vietnamese Readers (18-30) |

| **Reading Preference** | Isekai/Gaming, Romance undertones, Underdog-to-stronger progression |

| **Cultural Appeal** | Han Viet vocabulary appreciated (hắn, tao, mày, tụi mày) |

### VN.2 Genre Advantages for Vietnamese Market

- ✅ **Isekai + Gaming mechanics:** System status cards appeal to tech-savvy readers
- ✅ **Xuanhuan/Fantasy + Vietnamese cultural elements:** Dragon narratives resonate with cultural identity
- ✅ **Romance integrated with action:** Not standalone romance
- ✅ **"Vô dụng" (useless) protagonist patterns:** Readers root for underdogs

### VN.3 Action Scene Pacing for VN Readers

**Combat rhythm (short punchy):**
```
"Jack xông ra!"
"Bắn!"
"Ngọn lửa bùng lên."
```

**Chapter cliffhanger patterns:**
- "Bảy ngày…C"
- "Là nó. Thanatos."
- Leave a question in the reader's mind — no full resolution

### VN.4 Pronoun Consistency Checklist

- ❌ Using incorrect pronoun system (main characters should use tao/mày with friends, tôi/ngài with strangers)
- ❌ Inconsistent register (dialogue colloquial but inner thoughts too formal)
- ❌ Ignoring cliffhanger culture (chapter endings need hooks)

### VN.5 2026 Vietnamese Market Trend Signals

- 🔥 Isekai + Game system (status cards, level progression)
- 🔥 Underdog protagonist with hidden potential ("vô dụng" → strong)
- 🔥 Vietnamese vernacular in fantasy settings
- ❌ Outdated: Pure romance without plot, face-slapping without depth

## 2. Target Reader Profile Deep Construction

### 2.1 Four Basic Characteristic Dimensions

| Dimension | Core Question | Analysis Points |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Age Distribution** | Main reader age group? | 18-25 (students) / 25-35 (career) / 35+ (senior readers) |
| **Gender Ratio** | Male/female reader proportion? | Male (70%+ male) / Female (70%+ female) / Unisex |
| **Geographic Distribution** | Main source region? | First/second tier cities (high consumption) / Third/fourth tier (sinking market) |
| **Consumption Capacity** | Payment willingness? | High payment (monthly 100+) / Medium (30-100) / Low payment (<30) |

### 2.2 Reading Behavior Analysis

**Reading Scene Identification**:
- 🚇 Commute fragmented time (10-30 minutes) → Fast rhythm short stories
- 🌙 Before sleep relaxation period (30-60 minutes) → Light sweet romance
- ⏰ Weekend concentrated pursuit (1-3 hours) → Long in-depth stories

**Reading Device Preference**:
- 📱 Phone primary (90%+) → Layout needs small screen adaptation
- 💻 PC secondary (10%) → Long comment area interaction

**Pursuing Habits**:
- Daily updaters: Expect daily updates, drop at uncoupling
- Fatteners: Wait for completion or accumulate chapters to read
- Casual followers: Read when free, don't force updates

### 2.3 Content Preference Deep Analysis

**Core Cool Point Preferences** (by genre):

| Genre | Reader Expected Cool Points | Cool Point Frequency Requirement |
|-------|---------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Urban Rebirth** | Face-slap reversal, repeated face-slapping | One small climax every 3-5 chapters |
| **Fantasy Cultivation** | Cross-rank kills, realm breakthroughs | One major battle every 10 chapters |
| **System Flow** | Task rewards, ability improvement | At least one check-in/lottery per chapter |
| **Sweet Romance** | CEO doting, misunderstanding resolved | One sweet interaction every 2-3 chapters |

**Plot Pattern Preferences**:
- ✅ Progression flow: Clear growth path, numerical visualization
- ✅ Farming flow: Steady development, sense of achievement accumulation
- ✅ Ensemble flow: Multi-character perspective, rich plot
- ❌ Avoid: Dragging setup, repetitive plot, forced suffering protagonist

**Character Expectations**:
- Protagonist: Decisive > Hesitant saint
- Protagonist: High IQ > Dumb decisions
- Supporting: Three-dimensional vivid > Tool person stereotypes
- Antagonist: Reasonable motivation > Brainless feeding

**Innovation Boundaries**:
- ✅ Acceptable: Worldview micro-innovation, character anti-trope
- ✅ Must retain: Core cool point pattern, basic progression logic
- ❌ Restricted: Completely overturn reader expectations, reject conventional cool points

### 2.4 Reader Thunder Area Checklist (Must Avoid)

**Universal Thunder Areas**:
- ❌ Saint protagonist (weak, generous, scatter kindness)
- ❌ Dumb antagonists (brainless die, repeat mistakes)
- ❌ Dragging progression (ten-odd chapters only level up once)
- ❌ Harem brainless collection (female characters tool-ized)
- ❌ NTR plot (cuckold/betrayed)

**Vietnamese-Specific Considerations**:
- ❌ Using incorrect pronoun system (main characters should use tao/mày with friends, tôi/ngài with strangers)
- ❌ Inconsistent register (dialogue colloquial but inner thoughts too formal)
- ❌ Ignoring cliffhanger culture (chapter endings need hooks like "Bảy ngày…" pattern)
- ❌ Action scenes with long descriptive sentences instead of punchy short sentences

**Platform-Specific Thunder Areas**:
- Qidian: Excessive water words, illogical cheats
- Fanqie: Slow opening rhythm, no cool points in first three chapters
- Jinjiang: Uncorrect morals, gender discrimination

## 3. Market Trend Identification (2025 Sample)

### 3.1 Genre Lifecycle Judgment

**Lifecycle Stages**:

| Stage | Characteristics | Strategy |
|-------|------------------|----------|
| **Sprout** | Few trial waters, market acceptance unknown | High risk high reward, for innovators |
| **Growth** | Bestsellers appear, following works increase | Golden entry period, needs differentiation |
| **Maturity** | Market saturated, intense competition | Must have unique selling point |
| **Decline** | Reader aesthetic fatigue, new works hard to emerge | Avoid entry or deep innovation |

**2025 Genre Lifecycle Quick Reference**:
```
Sprout: AI awakening flow, metaverse adventure
Growth: Rules, female infinite flow
Mature: Urban rebirth, fantasy cultivation, system flow
Decline: Traditional harem, brainless face-slapping flow
```

### 3.2 Emerging Hot Elements

**2025 Trend Keywords Samples**:
- 🔥 **Ensemble multi-perspective**: No longer single protagonist, multi-line narration
- 🔥 **Reversal truth**: Suspense setup + truth reveal
- 🔥 **Rules**: SCP style, rule cracking
- 🔥 **Realistic themes**: Close to life, emotional resonance
- 🔥 **Female strong not in love**: Career line primary, emotional line secondary

**Elements Currently Becoming Outdated**:
- ❌ Single progression flow (pure number stacking)
- ❌ Brainless face-slapping (lacks IQ game)
- ❌ Harem mundane listing (female characters stereotyped)
- ❌ Dragging setup (too slow early)

### 3.3 Cross-Genre Fusion Trends

**Successful Combination Cases**:
- Urban + Fantasy = Urban cultivation (modern background + cultivation system)
- Mystery + Sci-Fi = Future crime reasoning
- History + Transmigration =lịch sử giả tưởng (crossing to another time)
- Sweet romance + Mystery = Solve case small sweet romance

**Fusion Principles**:
1. Main genre accounts for 70%, auxiliary genre 30%
2. The two produce chemical reaction, not simple patchwork
3. Target reader groups have intersection

## 4. Three-Sentence Work Positioning Method

### 4.1 First Sentence: Genre + Selling Point + Promise

**Template**:
```
This is a [genre] novel, core selling point is [unique selling point],
promising readers [core experience/emotional satisfaction].
```

**Example**:
```
This is an urban rebirth novel, core selling point is "antagonist redemption + reversal revenge",
promising readers ultimate reversal thrill and emotional release.
```

### 4.2 Second Sentence: Target Readers + Platform Strategy

**Template**:
```
Primarily targeting [age + gender] [platform] readers,
satisfying their expectations for [core needs].
```

**Example**:
```
Primarily targeting 25-35-year-old male Qidian readers,
satisfying their expectations for "high IQ + dense cool points + complete worldview".
```

### 4.3 Third Sentence: Differentiation + Competition Strategy

**Template**:
```
Compared to similar works, this work's differentiation lies in [unique point],
achieving market competition advantage through [specific strategy].
```

**Example**:
```
Compared to similar works, this work's differentiation lies in "multi-perspective ensemble + reversal truth",
achieving competition advantage through "one small reversal per chapter + one big reversal every 10 chapters" rhythm design.
```

**Vietnamese-Style Adaptation**:
For Vietnamese market positioning, adapt the three-sentence method with local patterns:
- First sentence: Emphasize underdog ("vô dụng" protagonist) or gaming system appeal
- Second sentence: Target 18-30 Vietnamese readers who appreciate Han Viet vernacular
- Third sentence: Differentiate through authentic Vietnamese expression with tao/mày dialogue in action scenes

## 5. Benchmark Work Analysis Method

### 5.1 Successful Case Studies

**Analysis Dimensions** (per work):
1. **Core selling point**: What is the most attractive element to readers?
2. **Differentiation**: How does it differ from same-genre works?
3. **Rhythm control**: How are cool points and climaxes set?
4. **Character highlights**: Features of protagonist/supporting/antagonist?
5. **Reader praise**: High-frequency praised points in comments?

**Example Analysis Table**:

| Work Name | Core Selling Point | Success Elements | Reader Praise Points |
|---------|------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| Work A | Antagonist redemption | Tight rhythm + dense reversals | "Reversals unexpected" |
| Work B | System check-in | Numerical visualization + sense of growth | "Cool point frequency high" |

### 5.2 Failed Case Pitfalls

**Common Failure Modes**:
- High start low end: Opening attractive, later collapses
- Rhythm loss: Early dragging or late rush
- Character collapse: Protagonist OOC
- Logical chaos: Settings contradict each other

**Analysis Points**:
- At which stage did it start declining?
- What is the direct cause of reader loss?
- Which mistakes could have been avoided in advance?

## 6. Platform Strategy and Publishing Plan

### 6.1 First Publishing Platform Decision Tree

```
Has paid reader foundation?
├─ Yes → Qidian/Jinjiang (subscription revenue high)
└─ No → Fanqie/Qimao (free traffic large)
    │
    └─ Genre leans toward power fantasy?
        ├─ Yes → Fanqie (algorithm recommendation fast)
        └─ No → Qimao (female realistic themes friendly)
```

### 6.2 Multi-Platform Strategy

**When to Choose Multi-Platform**:
- ✅ Wide genre audience (urban, sweet romance)
- ✅ Have energy to maintain multiple update channels
- ✅ Different platform content differentiation (e.g., Fanqie version rhythm faster)

**Multi-Platform Publishing Strategy**:
1. **First exclusive period**: Exclusively on one platform for 1-3 months
2. **Distribution period**: Authorize other platforms for simultaneous or delayed publishing
3. **Content differentiation**: Adjust rhythm/length based on platform
   - Qidian version: Deep worldview, long serialization
   - Fanqie version: Fast rhythm, concise chapters

### 6.3 Update Strategy

**Update Frequency Suggestions**:

| Platform | Suggested Frequency | Single Chapter Word Count | Reason |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------------|--------|
| Qidian | Daily update | 3000-4000 words | List competition needs stable updates |
| Fanqie | Daily 2-3 chapters | 2000-2500 words | Algorithm favors high-frequency updates |
| Jinjiang | Every other day or daily | 3000-5000 words | High reader acceptance |

**Free Chapter Quantity**:
- Qidian: 10-15 chapters (about 30-50K words)
- Fanqie: First 20-30 chapters free (attract traffic)
- Jinjiang: 5-10 chapters (quick into VIP)

### 6.4 Commercialization Path

**VIP Shelf Timing**:
- Word count standard: 30-50K words
- Chapter count: 10-20 chapters
- Data indicators: Favorites ≥500, recommendation votes ≥200

**Recommendation Application Strategy**:
1. **New book period** (0-30 days): Strive for new book list, signing recommendation
2. **Growth period** (1-3 months): Category recommendation, strong push
3. **Stabilization period** (3 months+): Quality recommendation, homepage recommendation

**Reader Operations Suggestions**:
- Regular interaction: Reply to comments at least once a week
- Chapter comments: Preview plot in advance, hook readers
- Welfare activities: Additional updates, lotteries, character votes

## 7. Risk Early Warning and Avoidance

### 7.1 Market Competition Risk

**Risk Description**:
- Too many similar works, head works squeeze market space
- New works hard to break through, traffic shunted

**Avoidance Strategies**:
1. **Differentiated selling point**: Avoid red ocean, find blue ocean niche
2. **Improve quality**: Comprehensive optimization of writing/logic/rhythm
3. **Precise positioning**: Deep cultivate vertical reader groups

### 7.2 Reader Demand Change Risk

**Risk Description**:
- Reader tastes change quickly, genres become outdated
- Target reader group too small, ceiling low

**Avoidance Strategies**:
1. **Continuous research**: Follow platform hot lists, reader comments
2. **Flexible adjustment**: Fine-tune plot direction based on feedback
3. **Diversified attempts**: Don't put all eggs in one basket

### 7.3 Platform Rules Risk

**Risk Description**:
- Platform algorithm adjusted, recommendation mechanism changes
- Audit standards tightened, content removed

**Avoidance Strategies**:
1. **Research rules**: Regularly check platform announcements
2. **Compliant creation**: Avoid sensitive genres and prohibited words
3. **Multi-platform backup**: Don't rely on single platform
