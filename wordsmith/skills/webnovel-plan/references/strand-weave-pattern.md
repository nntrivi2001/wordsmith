## Vietnamese Writing Patterns

> Adapted from STYLE_GUIDE_VN.md for review during chapter checking.

### VN.1 Pronoun Consistency Check

| Strand | Expected Register | Red Flags |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| Quest (battle/planning) | tao/mày between allies | Formal ngài/tôi between close allies |
| Fire (romance/emotion) | Mixed casual/formal based on relationship stage | Casual tone during formal romantic moments |
| Constellation (worldbuilding) | ngài/tôi for formal scenes | Colloquial slang in royal/nobility scenes |

### VN.2 Scene Rhythm Check

- **Quest chapters:** Short punchy sentences (2-4 words) for action beats
- **Fire chapters:** Longer descriptive sentences with emotional physicality
- **Constellation chapters:** Mix of formal register with scene-setting description

### VN.3 Cliffhanger Quality Check

- ✅ Ends with question/ambiguity, not resolution
- ✅ "Bảy ngày…" pattern (single sentence incomplete)
- ✅ Twist or reveal left open
- ❌ Full resolution at chapter end (kills reader momentum)

### VN.4 Transition Check

- `***` used for major strand switches (not within same strand)
- `—0o0—` for small scene shifts within same strand
- Character/POV changes follow `***` with new character name

### VN.5 Con Duong Ba Chu Patterns (Summary)

> Refer to shared strand-weave-pattern.md for full patterns.

**Strand distribution:**
| Strand | Ratio | Pacing | Cool-Point Type |
|--------|-------|--------|-----------------|
| Quest | 55-65% | Fast (70-80% short sentences) | Action punch, overlevel counter |
| Fire | 20-30% | Slow (70-80% long sentences) | Emotional beat, sweet exceeded |
| Constellation | 10-20% | Medium (50-60% long) | Status reveal, authority challenge |

**Cool-point density formula:**
```
Density = (Action_Punches × 1.0) + (Emotional_Beats × 0.8) + (Status_Reveals × 1.5)
```

**Chapter arc:** Opening (slow, 50-100 words) → Rising → Peak (fast, 1-5 word sentences) → Release → Hook (single dramatic cliffhanger sentence)

**Action sequence example:**
```
"Ầm!"
Hắn ta bị hất văng ra sau, đập vào tường.
"Bốp."
Một tiếng vỡ xương.
"Chết tiệt!"
```

**Cliffhanger patterns:** "Hắn ta nhìn thẳng vào mắt tôi. Đó là...", "Cô ấy biết. Nhưng mà...", "Thật ra, ta chưa bao giờ..."

## Vietnamese Writing Patterns (STYLE_GUIDE_VN.md Section 13)

### From Ta Dung Nhin Vo Lam Toang (Primary Source):
- Dialogue: "Nội dung" + action tag, no ——
- Inner thoughts: third-person narrative, "cậu" for self-reference
- Scene breaks: --- for major, *— Hết Chương —* for end
- First-person: cậu/mình in thoughts, tao/mày with close people
- Slang: vãi, cứt, bro (@ v @) in GenZ contexts

### Sentence Structure:
- Must have Subject + Predicate
- Connectors: và, nhưng, nên, vì, sau đó, rồi, thì, mà
- No fragmented sentences

### 8 Error Types:
1. Units: mét/cm/km/kg (NOT trượng/dặm/tấc/thốn/ly)
2. Vocabulary: match context
3. Punctuation: — single (NOT —— double)
4. Sentence: complete with subject+predicate
5. Spelling: ken két not kẽ kẽy
6. Connectors: required
7. Subject: explicit in descriptions
8. Natural Vietnamese words

### Cool-Point Placement:
- Every 500-800 words
- Fast-paced: 70-80% short sentences (1-7 words)
- Slow-paced: 70-80% long sentences (15+ words)

### Cliffhanger Patterns:
- Incomplete action ending
- System notification: "Keng!" + message
- Twist revelation
- Question left unanswered
