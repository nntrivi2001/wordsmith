# Genre/Volume-Level Rhythm Templates (Esports / Livestream / Cthulhu)

Used for `wordsmith-plan` Step 3/4: First set volume-level rhythm, then assign chapter-level distribution.

---

## Usage (Volume First, Then Chapter)

1. First determine this volume's main task (must be expressible in one sentence).
2. Select this volume's rhythm structure according to genre template (advancement type / confrontation type / recovery type).
3. Break volume-level goals into chapter batches, then write each chapter's hook and micro-delivery.

---

## Vietnamese Pacing Patterns

Based on STYLE_GUIDE_VN.md analysis of Vietnamese wordsmiths (Biên niên sử rồng, Useless hero from another world):

### Sentence Length Rhythm

| Scene Type | Sentence Style | Example |
|------------|---------------|---------|
| **Action/Combat** | Extremely short, strong verbs, minimal punctuation | `"Chết tiệt!" "Ầm!" "Bắn!"` |
| **Emotional/Sensory** | Long, descriptive, multiple clauses | `"Lâu đài Chyrse một buổi chiều lặng tuyết buồn tẻ lãng mạn..."` |
| **Climax Escalation** | Alternating short-long-short | Build tension through sentence compression |
| **Cliffhanger Ending** | 1-2 short sentences, left dangling | `"Bảy ngày…"` or twist left open |

### Transition Markers

| Marker | Usage | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| `***` | Major scene transition, time/location change | BNsr |
| `—0o0—` | Minor scene transition within chapter | UH |
| Explicit time | Flashback or time shift: "Sáu năm trước..." | BNsr |
| Explicit location | Scene change: "Ở trong khu rừng..." | UH |

### Pacing Rules

**Fast-paced (action/combat):**
- Short sentences, dồn dập
- Strong verbs, minimal clauses
- Sound effects interjected
- Close viewpoint, detailed

**Slow-paced (emotional/sensory):**
- Longer descriptive sentences
- Metaphors, sensory details
- Pause for reader digestion
- Atmospheric building

**Escalation pattern:**
- Tension climbs: sentences gradually shorten, punctuation drops
- Climax: extremely compressed sentences
- Release: return to descriptive pace

## Con Duong Ba Chu - Strand Distribution Patterns

### Strand Proportion Guidelines (Phân Bổ Strand)

Pattern từ **Con Duong Ba Chu** cho phân bổ ba strand trong volume:

| Strand | Proportion | Purpose | Content Type |
|--------|------------|---------|--------------|
| **Quest (Xương Sống)** | 55-65% | Drive plot forward | Combat, progression, achievement |
| **Fire (Lửa)** | 20-30% | Emotional investment | Relationships, trust, emotional moments |
| **Constellation (Ngân Đường)** | 10-20% | World expansion | Faction secrets, world rules, ecology |

**Critical Note**: Quest strand should NEVER exceed 70% (causes reader fatigue) or fall below 45% (plot stalls). Fire strand should be at least 20% to maintain emotional connection.

### Vietnamese Webnovel Strand Patterns

Based on **Con Duong Ba Chu** and BNsr/UH analysis:

**Quest-Dominant Structure (Tu Luyện, Đô Thị):**
```
Volume Structure:
- Quest: 60% (combat + progression)
- Fire: 25% (team bonds + romantic undertones)
- Constellation: 15% (sect politics + world reveals)
```

**Fire-Dominant Structure (Ngôn Luận, Đam Mỹ):**
```
Volume Structure:
- Quest: 45% (external goals)
- Fire: 35% (relationship development)
- Constellation: 20% (setting + supporting characters)
```

### Cool-Point Placement Every 500-800 Words

Pattern từ **Con Duong Ba Chu** cho placement điểm cool:

| Word Count | Cool-Point Type | Placement |
|------------|-----------------|----------|
| 500-800 words | Mini cool-point (micro-satisfaction) | Small face-slap, clever comeback |
| 1500-2000 words | Medium cool-point | Combat victory, status reveal |
| 3000+ words | Major cool-point | Milestone achievement, antagonist crush |

**Cool-Point Density Formula (Con Duong Ba Chu):**
```
Optimal density = 1 mini cool-point every 500-800 words
                 + 1 medium cool-point every 1500-2000 words
                 + 1 major cool-point per chapter (peak chapters)

Transition chapters can skip medium/major but MUST have mini cool-points
```

### Volume Pacing Structure (Vietnamese Webnovel Conventions)

Pattern từ **Con Duong Ba Chu** cho volume structure theo conventions Việt:

**Volume Opening (25% đầu):**
```
1-3 chapters: Establish volume's main conflict/goal
4-6 chapters: Plant seeds for mid-volume twist
7-10 chapters: First major confrontation (small victory or setback)
```

**Volume Middle (50% giữa):**
```
11-15 chapters: Escalate conflict, introduce new complications
16-20 chapters: Mid-volume reversal (protagonist faces crisis)
21-25 chapters: Recovery + preparation for climax
```

**Volume End (25% cuối):**
```
26-28 chapters: Final confrontation builds
29-30 chapters: Climax delivery + resolution
30+ chapters: Setup next volume's hook
```

### Con Duong Ba Chu Volume Checklist
- [ ] Volume has ONE main quest/goal (expressible in one sentence)
- [ ] Strand proportion balanced (Quest 55-65%, Fire 20-30%, Constellation 10-20%)
- [ ] Cool-point occurs at least every 500-800 words in action chapters
- [ ] Mid-volume reversal creates new tension
- [ ] Final chapters deliver promised payoff
- [ ] Last chapter ends with hook for next volume
- [ ] Fire strand maintains emotional investment throughout
- [ ] Quest strand progresses meaningfully (not filler)

## 1) Esports Volume Rhythm

### Recommended Strand Proportion
- Quest: 50-60% (competition progression, tactical execution, result delivery)
- Fire: 20-30% (team relationships, pressure management, trust rebuilding)
- Constellation: 15-20% (league ecology, team background, public opinion field)

### Volume Structure Template
- **Volume front 25%**: Lay out competition format, team weaknesses, this volume's hard goals.
- **Volume middle 50%**: Dual-line progression (match outcomes + team bonding), at least 1 comeback match.
- **Volume end 25%**: Key matches consecutive (semifinals/finals/promotion matches), deliver this volume's promises.

### Volume Check
- Each volume at least 1 chapter where "tactical decision changes the outcome."
- Each volume at least 1 "post-match public opinion reversal" or "evaluation system reversal."
- Within last 3 chapters of volume, must have clear competition result delivery.

---

## 2) Livestream Volume Rhythm

### Recommended Strand Proportion
- Quest: 45-55% (account goals, commercial goals, incident handling)
- Fire: 20-30% (team relationships, streamer psychological state, trust conflicts)
- Constellation: 20-30% (platform mechanisms, brand gaming, public opinion ecology)

### Volume Structure Template
- **Volume front 20%**: Set this volume's KPI ( follower growth/conversion/ranking/cooperation).
- **Volume middle 55%**: Continuous feedback loop (live feedback → decision → data change).
- **Volume end 25%**: Big event closure (PR battle/platform competition/business bet).

### Volume Check
- Each volume at least 2 quantifiable data leaps (e.g., online peak, conversion rate, ranking).
- Each volume at least 1 "negative public opinion → evidence reversal" closed loop.
- Volume end must output "next volume's bigger goal."

---

## 3) Cthulhu (Cosmic Horror) Volume Rhythm

### Recommended Strand Proportion
- Quest: 40-50% (investigation progression, rule cracking, risk decisions)
- Fire: 10-20% (relationship dissolution, trust and betrayal, psychological support)
- Constellation: 30-40% (rule system, faction truth, pollution spread)

### Volume Structure Template
- **Volume front 30%**: Deploy abnormal phenomena and first layer of rules.
- **Volume middle 45%**: Clue puzzle + partial cost delivery (cannot advance without cost).
- **Volume end 25%**: Stage truth reveal + higher threat opens.

### Volume Check
- Each volume at least 1 "rule recovery" and 1 "rule upgrade."
- Each volume at least 1 explicit cost landing (sanity/relationship/resource one of them).
- Volume end hook prioritizes "unclosed rule question," not pure scare.
