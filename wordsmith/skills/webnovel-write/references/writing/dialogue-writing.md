## 1. Vietnamese Pronoun System (Đại từ xưng hô)

### Pronoun Patterns by Relationship
| Pronoun | Usage | Context |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **mày/tao** | Equal/friends | Intimate, confident characters; between close friends or protagonist pairs |
| **tụi mày/bọn tao** | Group addressing | "You all" / "our group" |
| **hắn/hắn ta** | Third person male | Actions toward/opposing enemies |
| **ta/ngài** | Archaic/noble | Aristocratic characters, formal occasions |
| **tôi/ngài** | Polite/formal | Formal situations, strangers, official contexts |

### Emotion-Based Pronoun Shifts
- Normal mood → use "mày/mày"
- Angry → shift to "thằng khốn", "con nhóc", "thằng chó"
- Desperate/weak → "nó" instead of "hắn"

### Vietnamese Dialogue Characteristics
- **Colloquial dialogue**: Short, direct, everyday language
- **Literary narration**: Longer, metaphorical, descriptive
- **Inner monologue**: Uses normal language, less profanity than spoken words

### Con Duong Ba Chu Dialogue Format
**Em-dash dialogue (no closing quotation mark):**
```
—Cô ấy đi rồi.
—Anh biết.
—Vậy... chúng ta làm gì bây giờ?
```
- Standard Vietnamese format with em-dash
- Sometimes no closing quotation mark when dialogue is interrupted
- Short punchy dialogue in action scenes

**First-person inner monologue (Ta Duong Nhin Vo Lam Toang style):**
```
Cậu thầm nghĩ: "Chết tiệt! Lại là tên khốn đó!"
Nghĩ thôi, đừng nói ra.
Cậu cắn răng, lao tới.
```
- Character name + "thầm nghĩ" for inner thoughts (no quotation marks)
- Extensive use of "cậu" referring to self in first-person narration
- Inner monologue present without quotation marks

### Vietnamese Colloquial vs Literary Register
| Đời thường | Văn chương | Ghi chú |
|------------|------------|---------|
| đi đường | đường đi | Cùng nghĩa, khác trật tự |
| làm gì | làm chi | Hỏi mục đích |
| đâu | ở đâu | Nơi chốn |
| vậy | như vậy / thế | Cách nói |
| sao | như thế nào | Hỏi cách |

**Pattern đặc biệt:**
- Nhân vật bình thường dùng ngôn ngữ đời thường
- Khi suy nghĩ nội tâm hoặc mô tả cảnh quan thường dùng văn chương
- Đoạn hội thoại ngắn gọn, đời thường; đoạn độc thoại dùng văn chương hơn

**Pattern từ BNsr/UH:**
```

Jack nghiến răng. Kiếm bổ xuống, lần này Rosa bị hất văng về sau.
"Em xin lỗi, Jack! Xin anh nghe em. Nó là rồng cái..."
```

## 2. Subtext Five-Layer Analysis

### 1. Emotional subtext
Hidden emotions and feelings
```
Surface: "You came."
Subtext: Long-awaited / cold distance / surprised and uneasy
```

### 2. Motivational subtext
Hidden purposes and motives
```
Surface: "How have you been lately?"
Subtext: Probing / caring / finding topic / hinting
```

### 3. Relationship subtext
Hidden character relationships
```
Surface: "Hello, Director Wang."
Subtext: Respect / sarcasm / distance / courtesy
```

### 4. Plot subtext
Hidden plot development
```
Surface: "This matter, I will handle it."
Subtext: About to act / perfunctory / threat / promise
```

### 5. Thematic subtext
Hidden themes and ideas
```
Surface: "Everyone has their fate."
Subtext: Fatalism / helplessness / acceptance / irony
```

## 3. Subtext Type Library

| Type | Characteristics | Example | Subtext |
|------|-----------------|---------|---------|
| Probing type | Beating around the bush | "I heard you've been close with Director Li lately?" | Probing relationship / warning / jealousy |
| Hiding type | Vague | "That night? I was resting at home." | Hiding whereabouts / lying / avoiding |
| Hint type | More than meets the eye | "Some things, knowing too much isn't good." | Warning / threat / protection |
| Evasion type | Changing topic | "This... let's talk about it another day." | Avoiding / delaying / not wanting to face |

## 4. Dialogue Design Principles

### 1. Match character settings
- Language habits match identity
- Word style matches personality
- Speech style matches background

### 2. Advance plot development
- Deliver key information
- Create/upgrade conflict
- Reveal/hide truth

### 3. Show character relationships
- Address changes hint at relationship
- Tone changes hint at attitude
- Topic selection hints at intimacy

### 4. Contain subtext
- Surface meaning ≠ true intent
- Leave white space for reader thinking
- Reveal subtext through reactions

---

## 5. Dialogue Tone Design

| Tone | Performance | Subtext |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Gentle | Soft voice, slow speech | Caring / reassuring / seducing |
| Cold | Brief, flat | Distance / dissatisfaction / warning |
| Agitated | Urgent, repetitive | Anger / anxiety / excitement |
| Sarcastic | Anti-language, exaggerated | Dissatisfaction / mockery / attack |

**Silence design**:
- Meaning of silence: thinking / refusal / shock / forbearance
- Timing to break silence: key information / emotional turning point
- Silence description: action / expression / environment

## 6. Dialogue Rhythm Control

### Fast rhythm (battle/tense scenes)
```
"Kill!"
"Take this!"
"Break!"
Sword light flashed, blood splattered.
"You... how is this possible..."
"You're too weak."
```
**Characteristics**: Short sentences, fast action, crisp

### Slow rhythm (daily/setup scenes)
```
"Senior brother, do you think this realm opening will be very dangerous?"
Lin Tian pondered briefly: "Hard to say. According to ancient records, the last realm opening was three hundred years ago. Of the more than a hundred people who entered then, less than ten survived."
"Then... should we still go?"
"Go. Opportunity and danger coexist—this is the path of cultivation."
```
**Characteristics**: Long sentences, high information density, thinking pauses

---

## 7. Dialogue Combined with Action/Psychology

### Dialogue combined with action
```
❌ "Hello." he said.
✅ "Hello." He nodded slightly, but his gaze never left the document in his hands.
```

### Dialogue combined with psychology
```
"Nothing." she said.
But her clenched fists betrayed her emotions.
```

### Dialogue combined with environment
```
"Let's... break up."
The rain outside grew heavier, blurring the reflection on the glass.
```

## 8. Different Stage Dialogue Design

| Stage | Dialogue characteristics | Function |
|-------|-------------------------|---------|
| First meeting | Courtesy/probing | Establish first impression, lay relationship foreshadowing |
| Familiar | Daily interaction | Show personality, deepen relationship |
| Conflict | Arguing/confronting | Expose contradictions, drive turning point |
| Reconciliation | Explaining/apologizing | Resolve misunderstanding, upgrade relationship |

---

## 9. Dialogue Golden Ratio

- **Dialogue**: 40-50% (web novel mainstream)
- **Narration**: 30-40%
- **Psychological activity**: 10-20%

**Over-dialogue problem**: Waterlogged, readers will curse "can you stop the chitchat?"
**Under-dialogue problem**: All narration, lacking imagery

<errors>
❌ Information dumping ("You know, I came from Beijing three years ago, because my father was sick...") → ✅ Bury information scattered
❌ All characters sound the same → ✅ Personalize language
❌ Invalid dialogue ("Hello." "Hello." "Nice weather today." "Yes." ) → ✅ Every sentence has function

## Vietnamese Writing Patterns (STYLE_GUIDE_VN.md Section 13)

### From Ta Dung Nhin Vo Lam Toang (Primary Source):
- Dialogue: "Nội dung" + action tag, no ——
- Inner thoughts: third-person narrative, "cậu" for self-reference
- Scene breaks: --- for major, *— Hết Chương —* for end
- First-person: cậu/mình in thoughts, tao/mày with close people
- Slang: vãi, cứt, bro (@ v @) in GenZ contexts

### Sentence Structure:
- Must have Subject + Predicate
- Connectors: và, nhưng, nên, vì, sau đó, rồi, thì, mà
- No fragmented sentences

### 8 Error Types:
1. Units: mét/cm/km/kg (NOT trượng/dặm/tấc/thốn/ly)
2. Vocabulary: match context
3. Punctuation: — single (NOT —— double)
4. Sentence: complete with subject+predicate
5. Spelling: ken két not kẽ kẽy
6. Connectors: required
7. Subject: explicit in descriptions
8. Natural Vietnamese words

### Cool-Point Placement:
- Every 500-800 words
- Fast-paced: 70-80% short sentences (1-7 words)
- Slow-paced: 70-80% long sentences (15+ words)

### Cliffhanger Patterns:
- Incomplete action ending
- System notification: "Keng!" + message
- Twist revelation
- Question left unanswered
