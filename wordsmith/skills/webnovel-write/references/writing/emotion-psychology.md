## 1. Vietnamese Show-Don't-Tell Emotion Patterns

### Emotion Trigger Words (Biểu hiện cụ thể)
| Emotion | Physical Expression | Example |
|---------|---------------------|---------|
| Sợ (Fear) | run bần bật, mặt tái mét, đứng không vững | "Cơ thể hắn run bần bật khi nghĩ về..." (BNsr) |
| Giận (Anger) | nghiến răng, nổ gân, mắt nổ đom đóm | "Rosa nghiến răng, nắm chặt tay lại đến trắng bóc" |
| Tức giận cực độ | hét lên, khặc ặc, cười điên cuồng | "JACKKKK!!!!" (BNsr) |
| Buồn (Sadness) | nước mắt lã chã, cười méo, thở dài | "nước mắt lã chã" |
| Bất ngờ (Surprise) | tròn mắt, há hốc, giật mình | "tròn mắt" |
| Xúc động (Moved) | nghẹn bứ, run lên, khóc nức nở | "nghẹn bứ" |

### Show-Don't-Tell Examples
```
❌ "Cô ấy rất giận."
✅ "Rosa nghiến răng, nắm chặt tay lại đến trắng bóc. Mắt cô nổ đom đóm."

❌ "Endou rất sợ hãi"
✅ "Khuôn mặt của Endou trở nên tái mét hẳn đi, cơ thể hắn run bần bật khi nghĩ về con quái vật đội lốt người đó."
```

### Unreliable Narrator Pattern (UH style)
Tanaka's inner monologue with self-deprecating humor:
```
'Ừm, có vẻ ngon nghệ đấy!' - Tanaka nghĩ khi nhìn tiền
Nhưng thực tế cậu không hiểu giá trị đồng xu

"Khoan, mình đã làm được gì trong suốt mấy ngày qua không? Đếch làm cái đinh gì cả!!!!"
```

### Con Duong Ba Chu Inner Thoughts Pattern
**Without quotation marks, introduced by character name + "thầm nghĩ":**
```
Anh ta nghiến răng.
Virus thầm nghĩ: "Chết tiệt! Lại là cảnh này!"
Hắn ta cười.
"Tao không có thời gian cho mày."
Cậu — kẻ đứng ngoài — run bần bật.
```
- Inner thoughts present without quotation marks
- Character name + "thầm nghĩ" introduces internal monologue
- First-person narration using "cậu" for self-reference

### First-Person Inner Monologue (Ta Duong Nhin Vo Lam Toang style)
```
Cậu thầm nghĩ: "Mình không nên ở đây."
Nhưng chân cậu đã di chuyển.
"Chạy thôi!" — cậu tự nhủ
Nhưng không có đường ra.
```
- Extensive use of "cậu" referring to self in first-person
- Short internal commentary interspersed with action
- Mixed colloquial and literary registers

### Emotion-Based Pronoun Shifts
Đại từ xưng hô thay đổi theo cảm xúc:
| Tình trạng | Ngôi xưng | Ví dụ |
|------------|-----------|-------|
| Bình thường | mày/mày | "Mày nghĩ tao sợ mày à!" (UH Chương 1) |
| Giận dữ | thằng khốn, con nhóc | "Hắn ta dám..." |
| Khinh bỉ | thằng chó, đồ khốn | "Thằng chó đó mà dám..." (UH Chương 3) |
| Tuyệt vọng/thua kém | nó thay vì hắn | Nhân vật yếu thường bị gọi "nó" |

**Pattern quan trọng:**
- Nhân vật chính (Rosa, Jack) dùng "tao-mày" với nhau nhưng "tôi-ngài" với người lạ
- Khi tâm trạng điên tiết: kết hợp nhiều từ tục: "Mẹ nó! Chết tiệt! Thằng khốn!"
- Nội tâm suy nghĩ dùng ngôn ngữ bình thường, ít tục tĩu hơn lời nói

## 2. Three Layers of Psychological Description

### Conscious level
Psychological activities the character is aware of
```
He knows he shouldn't think this way, but he can't help...
```

### Subconscious level
Psychological activities the character vaguely feels but doesn't want to face
```
She told herself they were just friends, yet she couldn't help but glance in his direction.
```

### Unconscious leakage
Psychological disclosure the character isn't aware of
```
"I'm fine." she said, but her clenched fists betrayed her.
```

## 3. Five Psychological Description Techniques

### 1. Direct inner monologue
Psychological display in key scenes
```
"Impossible!" His mind reeled with shock. "How can he have this kind of power?"
```
**Usage principle**: Use at key emotional nodes, no more than 3 sentences per passage

### 2. Indirect psychological description
Show through behavior, expression, dialogue
```
Her fingers unconsciously twisted her sleeve, gaze dodging, avoiding his eyes.
```

### 3. Sensory-triggered psychology
Vision, hearing, touch, smell triggering
```
The familiar perfume scent drifted over, his heart suddenly tightened—it's her.
```

### 4. Dream refraction of psychology
Dream symbolism and metaphor
```
He dreamed that scene again—her turning and walking away, him chasing desperately but never catching up.
```

### 5. Free association
Thought jumps revealing deeper psychology
```
Looking at the rain outside the window, he thought of that day, of her tears, of his own cowardice...
```

## 4. Emotional Change Curves

### Single emotion progression
```
Uneasy → worried → fearful → despairing
Affection → heart racing → liking → deep love
```

### Emotional turning points
```
Anger → shock → understanding → relief
Expectation → disappointment → anger → indifference
```

### Emotional contradiction
```
Love and hate intertwined
Trust and suspicion coexisting
Longing and fear together
```

## 5. Emotional Intensity Levels

| Level | Characteristics | Word count | Description method |
|-------|-----------------|------------|-------------------|
| Level 1 | Daily minor fluctuation | 10 words | Mention in passing |
| Level 2 | Obvious emotion | 30 words | Simple description |
| Level 3 | Strong emotion | 50 words | External + internal combined |
| Level 4 | Emotional explosion | 100-200 words | Chapter climax |

**Level 1 example**: Lin Tian somewhat helplessly shook his head.

**Level 2 example**: Lin Tian frowned, feeling somewhat unhappy. This Ye Liangchen really is too arrogant.

**Level 3 example**:
```
Lin Tian gripped his sword hilt, knuckles white.
(Endure! Have to endure! It's not the time yet!)
He took a deep breath, forcefully suppressing the fury in his heart.
```

**Level 4 example**:
```
"MASTER!!!"
Lin Tian's scream, voice heart-wrenching.
He knelt in a pool of blood, hands trembling as he cradled his master's body.
Tears flowed unceasingly.
Why……
Why am I so weak?!
Why did I have to watch master die, unable to do anything?!
"I swear..." Lin Tian raised his head, eyes full of bloodshot. "Blood God Sect, I will annihilate your entire clan!"
```

## 6. Inner Conflict Design

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Desire vs morality | What you want vs what you should do | Want revenge but know it's wrong |
| Reason vs emotion | Rational judgment vs emotional impulse | Know should let go but can't bear to |
| Self vs others | Personal interest vs others' interest | Save yourself or save others |
| Past vs present | Past experiences vs present choices | Past hurt affecting present choices |

**Conflict expression**: Hesitation → endless struggle → self-persuasion → final choice

## 7. Core Emotion Description Templates

### Anger (most commonly used)
**Trigger**: Humiliation, family member killed, betrayal
```
【External】Lin's face was terrifyingly dark, eyes seemingly about to spew fire. Both fists clenched tight, body slightly trembling.
【Internal】(/I want to kill him!)
【Action】"Die!" Lin Tian surged forward, slashing with his sword!
```

### Sadness (essential for knife chapters)
**Trigger**: Family death, lover leaving
```
【External】Lin Tian knelt on the ground, shoulders trembling violently. Tears blurred his vision.
【Internal】(/Master... I'm sorry... it's all my fault...)
【Turn】He raised his head, eyes gradually turning grief into hatred. "I will avenge you..."
```
**Note**: Sadness cannot last too long, must quickly turn to "revenge determination"

### Fear (creating oppression)
**Trigger**: Encountering a rival far beyond one's level
```
【External】Cold sweat dripped from Lin Tian's forehead, back growing cold. This aura... it's too terrifying!
【Internal】(/No, can't fight head-on! Have to run!)
【Action】He didn't hesitate, turned and ran. A living person has options!
```

### Despair (ultimate suffering point)
**Trigger**: Dead end, all betrayed
```
【External】Lin Tian sat helplessly on the ground, eyes hollow. Everyone betrayed him...
【Internal】(/Why... why did it turn out like this...)
【Turn】Right at that moment, a voice sounded: "Young man, do you want to become stronger?"
```
**Note**: Despair at most 1-2 chapters, exists to contrast subsequent counterattack

## 8. Progressive Emotion Description

### Wrong example (emotional jump)
```
Chapter 10: Lin Tian is in good mood, joking with senior brother.
Chapter 11: Lin Tian learns master was killed, anger reaches extreme, swears revenge!
**Problem**: Too sudden, no foreshadowing

### Correct example (progressive)
```
Chapter 10: Lin Tian notices master's recent movements are strange, somewhat worried.
Chapter 11: Master tidies up affairs before leaving, Lin Tian feels uneasy.
Chapter 12: Lin Tian learns master is in danger, hurries to help.
Chapter 13: Lin Tian arrives, master already dead, sorrow + anger explodes!
```

## Vietnamese Writing Patterns (STYLE_GUIDE_VN.md Section 13)

### From Ta Dung Nhin Vo Lam Toang (Primary Source):
- Dialogue: "Nội dung" + action tag, no ——
- Inner thoughts: third-person narrative, "cậu" for self-reference
- Scene breaks: --- for major, *— Hết Chương —* for end
- First-person: cậu/mình in thoughts, tao/mày with close people
- Slang: vãi, cứt, bro (@ v @) in GenZ contexts

### Sentence Structure:
- Must have Subject + Predicate
- Connectors: và, nhưng, nên, vì, sau đó, rồi, thì, mà
- No fragmented sentences

### 8 Error Types:
1. Units: mét/cm/km/kg (NOT trượng/dặm/tấc/thốn/ly)
2. Vocabulary: match context
3. Punctuation: — single (NOT —— double)
4. Sentence: complete with subject+predicate
5. Spelling: ken két not kẽ kẽy
6. Connectors: required
7. Subject: explicit in descriptions
8. Natural Vietnamese words

### Cool-Point Placement:
- Every 500-800 words
- Fast-paced: 70-80% short sentences (1-7 words)
- Slow-paced: 70-80% long sentences (15+ words)

### Cliffhanger Patterns:
- Incomplete action ending
- System notification: "Keng!" + message
- Twist revelation
- Question left unanswered

<errors>
❌ Over-explaining ("He was angry because she deceived him, and he hates being deceived the most, because when he was a child...") → ✅ Leave white space for readers to feel
❌ Psychology and behavior contradiction ("She's happy inside but angry expression" without explanation) → ✅ Need reasonable explanation or consistency
❌ All characters the same ("all characters have same psychological activity pattern") → ✅ Personalize psychological description
❌ Disconnected from character setting ("calm, rational character suddenly emotional with no foreshadowing") → ✅ Need reasonable foreshadowing
❌ Over-sentimental ("he cried for three days and three nights") → ✅ Moderate restraint has more power
❌ Emotional jump (happy last chapter, anger explodes this chapter) → ✅ Progressive emotional development
</errors>
