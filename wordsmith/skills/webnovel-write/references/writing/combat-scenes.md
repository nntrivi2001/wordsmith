## 1. Vietnamese Combat Writing Patterns

### Sentence Rhythm for Combat
**Short punchy sentences (action scenes):**
```
"Jack xông ra!" (BNsr Chương 1)
"Rồi cậu cười."
"Một con Goblin rơi xuống." (UH Chương 4)
```

**Sound effects for impact:**
```
"Ầm!"
"Bắn!"
"Chết tiệt!"
"Ngọn lửa bùng lên."
```

**Con Duong Ba Chu combat rhythm (fragmented bursts):**
```
Ầm...!
Tia lửa bắn tung tóe. Jack lăn qua bên trái, kiếm chém ngang.
Hắn ta — kẻ mặc giáp đen — gầm lên một tiếng dữ dội.
"Bỏ chạy thôi! Đây là bẫy!"
```
- Combat alternates between short explosive bursts and longer descriptive passages
- Sound effects like "Ầm...!" create impact rhythm
- Action fragments interspersed with strategic inner monologue

**System Notifications (Keng! style):**
```
【Keng!】
[System Alert: Enemy defeated! +50 EXP]
【Ding! Achievement unlocked: First Blood】
```
- **Con Duong Ba Chu style**: "Keng!" exclamation with brackets
- System notifications add gaming element for isekai/rebirth stories
- Notifications should feel impactful, not routine

**Cliffhanger Combat Endings:**
```
Một kiếm quyết. Chỉ một.
Hắn ta ngã xuống.
"Đừng tưởng bở... Thằng khốn..."
Câu nói còn chưa dứt, bóng đen đã lao tới.
[Virus đang tiếp cận...]
```
- Single dramatic sentence ending
- Hook placement that creates curiosity
- System warnings as cliffhangers

**Fast-paced combat structure (BNsr style):**
```
"Kiếm chạm kiếm tóe lửa. Jack bị hất văng về phía sau, lại lao lên.
-Đánh trả đi - anh rít lên.
-Nghe em nói đã!"
```

### Vietnamese Combat Pacing
- Opening: Slow, descriptive atmosphere
- Combat: Short sentences, strong verbs, minimal punctuation
- Climax: Ultra-short, often single words or sound effects
- Conclusion: Brief resolution

### Formality Levels in Combat Dialogue
| Mức độ | Ngôi xưng | Ngữ cảnh |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Cao nhất | ngài, tôi | Vua, quý tộc, nghi lễ |
| Cao | tôi, ông, bà | Người lạ, công việc |
| Bình đẳng | mày, tao | Bạn bè thân, đồng đội |
| Thấp | thằng/kẻ (khiếm khuyết) | Đối thủ, khinh bỉ |

**Lưu ý quan trọng:**
- Ngôn ngữ bạn bè thân: "mày/tao" nhưng khi giận chuyển sang "thằng chó"
- Nhân vật yếu thường bị xưng "nó" thay vì "hắn"
- Khi tâm trạng điên tiết: kết hợp nhiều từ tục: "Mẹ nó! Chết tiệt! Thằng khốn!"

### Isekai/Gaming Combat Flow (UH style)
1. See enemy → Check status card (Giám định)
2. Assess strength
3. Tactical decision
4. Action
5. Result + loot/exp

**Status card example:**
```

(Tình trạng: Suy dinh dưỡng)

-Tuổi: 17

-Cấp: 1

-Kĩ năng:
+Cơ Địa Của Kẻ Vô Dụng (Độc nhất)
```

## 2. Five-Phase Structure

| Phase | Proportion | Content | Pace |
|-------|------------|---------|------|
| Opening | 15-20% | Standoff, probing, environmental atmosphere, psychological warfare | Slow |
| Probing | 20-25% | Initial exchange, strength testing, ability display | Gradually faster |
| Escalation | 30-40% | Full engagement, trump cards revealed, battle shift | Fast |
| Climax | 15-20% | Key turning point, decisive blow, emotional peak | Ultra fast |
| Conclusion | 10-15% | Victory determined, aftermath, atmosphere settling | Slow |

**Combat Types and Word Counts**:

| Type | Words | Pace | Cool Point |
|------|-------|------|------------|
| One-hit kill | 50-100 | Ultra fast | Overwhelming thrill |
| Stomp | 200-300 | Fast | Face-slapping |
| Evenly matched | 500-800 | Medium | Comeback |
| Tough battle | 800-1500 | Slow | Explosive reversal |
| Major battle | 2000+ | Multi-stage | Multiple climaxes |

## 3. Pace Variation Control

**Fast pace (intense exchange)**: Short sentences + dense action + quick cuts
```
Xiao Yan moved.
One punch.
Wang Hao stumbled back three steps.
Another punch.
The sound of bone crushing.
```

**Slow pace (confrontational charging)**: Long sentences + detailed description + psychological activity
```
Xiao Yan took a deep breath, feeling the flow of battle qi within his body. He knew this next strike would decide everything. Opponent Wang Hao was also charging up, the air thick with suffocating pressure.
```

**Pace curve**:
```
Slow (confrontation) → Fast (exchange) → Slow (breathing) → Fast (explosion) → Slow (conclusion)
```

## 4. Shot Composition Techniques

| Shot type | Description focus | Applicable scene |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Wide shot | Overall battle picture, battlefield panorama, force comparison | Opening, large-scale battle |
| Close-up | Detailed action and expressions, micro-expressions, muscle tension | Key actions, emotional expression |
| Close-up shot | Key moment magnification (fist contact, weapon collision) | Decisive moment |

**Shot composition example**:
```
【Wide shot】Two figures stood confrontation on the mountaintop, wind howling.
【Close-up】A gleam flashed in Xiao Yan's eyes, the corner of his mouth slightly raised.
【Close-up shot】His fist clenched, knuckles whitening.
【Wide shot】The next instant, both figures vanished simultaneously, the mountaintop exploding into a massive crater.
```

## 5. Sensory Description

| Sense | Description focus | Example |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| Visual | Action trajectory, effect lights, environmental changes | Sword light like a dragon, flames soaring |
| Auditory | Whooshing fists, weapon collision, bone breaking | A crisp "crack" sound |
| Tactile | Fist impact, pain transmission, air wave impact | Vibration of bone fracturing |
| Olfactory | Blood smell, burning smell, sweat smell | Blood fills the air |

**Comprehensive example**:
```
At the moment his fist struck Wang Hao's chest, Xiao Yan felt the vibration of bone fracturing【tactile】.
A crisp "crack"【auditory】, Wang Hao's body flew like a broken kite【visual】.
A faint smell of blood fills the air【olfactory】.
```

## 6. Psychological Description Coordination

**Combat psychology change curve**:
```
Confidence → Alert → Shock → Fear → Despair
or
Nervous → Calm → Excited → Fanatical → Satisfied
```

**Inner monologue timing**:
- Before key decisions
- When discovering opponent's trump card
- Before using one's own trump card
- When victory/defeat is already determined

**Psychological description example**:
```
"Impossible!" Wang Hao's mind reeled with shock. "He's clearly just a battle cultivator, how can he have this kind of power?"
Fear surged like a tide—he regretted provoking this "trash" for the first time.
```

---

## 7. Audience Reaction (mandatory)

Use bystander reactions to amplify the protagonist's awesomeness and enhance the cool factor:

```
The entire arena was stunned.
"This... how is this possible!"
"He actually one-shotted..."
Everyone looked at each other, disbelief evident in their eyes.
```

**Audience reaction types**:
- Stunned type: Pupils dilate, jaws drop
- Fearful type: Step back, cold sweat, legs go weak
- Adoring type: Eyes light up, cheers
- Jealous type: Face turns green, grinds teeth

---

## 8. Combat Type Templates

**1v1 duel**: Confrontation → Probing → Exchange → Trump cards → Decisive victory

**Group battle**: Panorama → Focus on protagonist → Cut to supporting characters → Key turning point → Wrap up

**Cross-level challenge**: Suppression → Struggle → Turn → Explosion → Reversal

**Team coordination**: Division of labor → Coordination → Crisis → Synergy → Victory

---

## 9. Combat Logic Consistency

**Checklist**:
- [ ] Combat process matches both parties' strength settings
- [ ] Combat result is reasonable and convincing
- [ ] Combat details align with world rules
- [ ] Ability usage follows established limitations
- [ ] Physical/stamina consumption is reasonable

**Common problems**:
- ❌ Cross-level battle without reasonable explanation
- ❌ Ability suddenly exceeds settings
- ❌ Infinite stamina/no consumption
- ❌ Opponent's intelligence suddenly drops

## Vietnamese Writing Patterns (STYLE_GUIDE_VN.md Section 13)

### From Ta Dung Nhin Vo Lam Toang (Primary Source):
- Dialogue: "Nội dung" + action tag, no ——
- Inner thoughts: third-person narrative, "cậu" for self-reference
- Scene breaks: --- for major, *— Hết Chương —* for end
- First-person: cậu/mình in thoughts, tao/mày with close people
- Slang: vãi, cứt, bro (@ v @) in GenZ contexts

### Sentence Structure:
- Must have Subject + Predicate
- Connectors: và, nhưng, nên, vì, sau đó, rồi, thì, mà
- No fragmented sentences

### 8 Error Types:
1. Units: mét/cm/km/kg (NOT trượng/dặm/tấc/thốn/ly)
2. Vocabulary: match context
3. Punctuation: — single (NOT —— double)
4. Sentence: complete with subject+predicate
5. Spelling: ken két not kẽ kẽy
6. Connectors: required
7. Subject: explicit in descriptions
8. Natural Vietnamese words

### Cool-Point Placement:
- Every 500-800 words
- Fast-paced: 70-80% short sentences (1-7 words)
- Slow-paced: 70-80% long sentences (15+ words)

### Cliffhanger Patterns:
- Incomplete action ending
- System notification: "Keng!" + message
- Twist revelation
- Question left unanswered
