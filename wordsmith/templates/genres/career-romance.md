# Workplace Romance Genre Template

> **Core Appeal**: Workplace gaming + Growth promotion + Emotional balance. Find your own balance point on the scale of career and love.

---

## Creative Constraints (Optional)
- Recommended Pack: Pack F09
- Universal Overlay: Pack U02

## 1. Core Sub-Genres

### Office Romance Flow
- **Feature**: Romance between superior-subordinate/colleagues
- **Core Satisfaction**: Secret love becomes real, identity reversal, sweet daily life
- **Representative Works**: 《Shan Shan Lai Le》《He Yi Sheng Xiao Mo》《Ni Shi Wo De Rong Yao》

### Workplace Counterattack Flow
- **Feature**: Workplace newbie grows into elite
- **Core Satisfaction**: Ability face-slapping, promotion counterattack, career and love both win
- **Representative Works**: 《Huan Le Song》《Wo De Qian Ban Sheng》《Dou Tai Hao》

### Business War Gaming Flow
- **Feature**: Love and hate in commercial competition
- **Core Satisfaction**: Business war victory, opponents submit, strong-strong alliance
- **Representative Works**: 《Chuang Ye Shi Dai》《Lie Chang》《Jing Ying Lu Shi》

### Industry Elite Flow
- **Feature**: Professional stories in specific industries
- **Core Satisfaction**: Professional crush, industry recognition, legendary in the industry
- **Representative Works**: 《Wai Ke Feng Yun》《Fa Yi Qin Ming》《Fan Yi Guan》

## 2. Worldview: Workplace Sociology

### Workplace Levels
| Level | Feature | Power |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Executive level | Decision power | Strategic direction |
| Middle level | Execution power | Department management |
| Front-line supervisor | Supervision power | Team management |
| Ordinary employee | Executor | Specific work |
| Intern | Learner | Run errands |

### Industry Characteristics
| Industry | Feature | Core Conflict |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| Finance | High pressure high pay | Performance vs ethics |
| Internet | Fast pace | Innovation vs stability |
| Lawyer | Rigorous logic | Justice vs interest |
| Medical | Life and death | Save people vs rules |
| Media | Public opinion influence | Truth vs traffic |

### Workplace Resources
| Resource | Description | Acquisition Method |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Connections | Relationship network | Social/projects |
| Performance | Work results | Effort/opportunity |
| Ability | Professional skills | Learning/practice |
| Reputation | Workplace reputation | Accumulation/maintenance |
| Backer | Superior support | Performance/side-taking |

## 3. Workplace System Design

### Promotion Path
```
Professional path:
Intern → Junior → Mid-level → Senior → Expert → Chief

Management path:
Employee → Team leader → Supervisor → Manager → Director → VP → CEO

Entrepreneurship path:
Employee → Partner → Founder → Listed company boss
```

### Workplace Gaming
| Type | Method | Risk |
|------|--------|------|
| Performance competition | Compete by ability | Low |
| Resource competition | Grab projects | Medium |
| Side-taking | Choose backers | High |
| Office politics | Form factions | Extremely high |

### Workplace Rules
```
Explicit rules:
- Performance evaluation
- Promotion system
- Reward and punishment mechanism

Unspoken rules:
- Factional alignment
- Social skills
- Information gap
```

## 4. Classic Satisfaction Formulas

### Ability Face-Slap
- **Scene**: Prove self after being questioned
- **Satisfaction**: Professional crush, opponent speechless
- **Key**: Must have professional details to support

### Promotion Counterattack
- **Scene**: Rise from bottom to high position
- **Satisfaction**: Those who looked down on MC now must look up
- **Key**: Must have growth process

### Business War Victory
- **Scene**: Win key project/negotiation
- **Satisfaction**: Defeat opponent, gain recognition
- **Example**: Win big client with creative proposal

### Identity Reversal
- **Scene**: Hidden identity revealed
- **Satisfaction**: Turns out to be big boss/silver spoon
- **Key**: Must have foreshadowing, can't be too abrupt

## 5. Outline Structure (800K Words Standard)

### Volume 1: Enter Workplace (Chapters 1-50)
- **Core**: Enter workplace, adapt to environment
- **Status**: Newcomer/bottom
- **Climax**: First project success

### Volume 2: Growth (Chapters 51-120)
- **Core**: Ability improves, romance sprouting
- **Status**: Backbone employee
- **Climax**: Get promotion opportunity

### Volume 3: Test (Chapters 121-200)
- **Core**: Dual tests of career and emotion
- **Status**: Middle management
- **Climax**: Survive crisis

### Volume 4: Breakthrough (Chapters 201-280)
- **Core**: Career breakthrough, emotion confirmed
- **Status**: High-level/partner
- **Climax**: Career peak

### Volume 5: Fulfillment (Chapters 281-350)
- **Core**: Career and love both win
- **Status**: Industry elite
- **Climax**: Satisfying ending

## 7. Industry Description Techniques

### Creating Professional Feel
- Use industry terms but must explain
- Work process must be reasonable
- Workplace rules must be realistic

### Common Industry Settings
| Industry | Core Scenes | Professional Elements |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Finance | Trading/roadshow | Valuation/due diligence |
| Lawyer | Court/negotiation | Cases/clauses |
| Medical | Surgery/consultation | Cases/surgical methods |
| Internet | Product/financing | Users/data |
| Media | Interview/release | Topics/traffic |

### Workplace Atmosphere
```
Daily scenes:
- Meeting room gaming
- Gossip by water cooler
- Overtime on projects
- Team building activities

Key scenes:
- Presentation defense
- Project report
- Business negotiation
- Crisis PR
```

## 8. Common Pitfalls

### Must Avoid
- ❌ Workplace description too fake
- ❌ Only romance no work
- ❌ Promotion too fast and smooth
- ❌ Professional knowledge wrong

### Recommended Approach
- ✅ Workplace details realistic
- ✅ Career and emotion balanced
- ✅ Promotion has process
- ✅ Professional knowledge accurate
