# Lovecraftian Genre Template

> **Core Appeal**: Unnameable + Reason collapse + Truth at a cost. Reader satisfaction comes from "getting close to truth but unable to bear it."

---

## Creative Constraints (Optional)
- Recommended Pack: Pack M21
- Universal Overlay: Pack U04

## 1. Core Sub-Genres

### Investigation File Flow
- **Feature**: Case logs/journals/manuscripts advance the story, jigsaw narrative
- **Core Satisfaction**: Clue closure, truth revealed, evidence reversal
- **Adaptation**: Mystery readers, rational deduction emphasis

### Ritual Out of Control Flow
- **Feature**: Taboo ritual triggers catastrophe, consequences escalate layer by layer
- **Core Satisfaction**: Stopping out-of-control, cost exchange, partial victory
- **Adaptation**: Strong conflict, strong crisis

### Old One Invasion Flow
- **Feature**: Real-world order polluted by ancient god rules
- **Core Satisfaction**: Establishing new survival rules in crumbling order
- **Adaptation**: Worldview-type long storyline

### Sanity Edge Flow
- **Feature**: MC maintains action capability while sanity decreases
- **Core Satisfaction**: Using tiny reason to exchange for key victory
- **Adaptation**: Strong character psychological portrayal

---

## 2. Worldview: Horror Rules

### Truth Levels
| Level | Information | Cost |
|-------|-------------|------|
| Surface anomaly | Explainable deviation | Fear and misjudgment |
| Middle pollution | Rule distortion | Sanity decline, relationship breakdown |
| Deep truth | Cosmic hostility | Identity collapse, irreversible loss |

### Power Layers
- **Investigator organization**: Trying to control anomaly spread
- **Secret cult**: Pursuing ancient god descent or sacrifice benefits
- **Neutral bystander**: Holds intelligence but refuses to bear consequences
- **Polluted individuals**: Affected people or places

---

## 3. Classic Satisfaction Formulas

### Rule Decoding
- **Scene**: Mysterious room/ruins/ritual text cannot be understood
- **Satisfaction**: MC decodes key rules through details
- **Key**: Rules must be consistently applied throughout, verifiable

### Cost Victory
- **Scene**: Cannot "clear without damage"
- **Satisfaction**: MC exchanges clear cost for phased victory
- **Key**: Cost must actually affect subsequent plot

### Fake Truth Reversal
- **Scene**: Seems concluded, actually just deeper entrance
- **Satisfaction**: Recycle previous details, complete secondary reversal
- **Key**: Reversal doesn't rely on "suddenly told", relies on clue recycling

### Sanity Line Defense
- **Scene**: MC on brink of collapse
- **Satisfaction**: Forces maintenance of sanity through obsession or promise to complete mission
- **Key**: Emotional anchor must be foreshadowed early

## 4. Outline Structure (1.8 Million Words Reference)

### Volume 1: Abnormal Sound (Chapters 1-70)
- **Core**: Discover anomaly and first rule
- **Climax**: First time leaving polluted area alive

### Volume 2: Deep Dive (Chapters 71-180)
- **Core**: Investigation network expands, organization vs cult confrontation
- **Climax**: Key character loses pollution control

### Volume 3: Rift (Chapters 181-320)
- **Core**: World partial rule rewritten
- **Climax**: Cost-type victory, core city preserved

### Volume 4: Reset (Chapters 321-500)
- **Core**: MC faces deep truth
- **Climax**: Sacrifice and sealing, reconstruct new order

---

## 5. Common Pitfalls

### Must Avoid
- ❌ Only writing "scary descriptions", no rule logic
- ❌ Ancient god settings almighty, making plot unsolvable
- ❌ Cost said to exist but actually has no consequences
- ❌ Reversals all hard-revealed in final chapter

### Recommended Approach
- ✅ Each anomaly gives at least 1 verifiable rule
- ✅ MC pays concrete cost each time pushing forward
- ✅ Use "partial victory" to maintain serial satisfaction
- ✅ Before reversal ensure traceable clues
