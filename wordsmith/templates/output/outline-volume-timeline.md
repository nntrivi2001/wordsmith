## Time Jump Rules

### Permitted Time Advances
- Same-day continuous: No special explanation needed
- Overnight (6-12 hours): Needs 1-2 transition sentences (e.g., "The night passed uneventfully" / "They rested overnight")
- Cross-day (1-3 days): Needs a transition paragraph or transition chapter explaining what happened in between
- Large jump (>3 days): Must have a transition chapter, or an explicit time marker ("Three days later")

### Prohibited Time Operations
- ❌ Time reversal (unless explicitly a flashback/memory)
- ❌ Countdown jumps (D-5 cannot jump directly to D-2)
- ❌ Large advances without explanation

## Vietnamese Narrative Pacing Notes

When setting timeline beats for Vietnamese wordsmiths, apply these conventions:

### Scene Pacing by Type

| Scene Type | Pacing | Sentence Length |
|------------|--------|-----------------|
| Action/Combat | Fast | Short sentences, minimal punctuation, punchy verbs |
| Emotional/Sensory | Slow | Long descriptive, more commas, inner monologue |
| Dialogue | Moderate | Conversational, natural pauses using `...` and `—` |
| Cliffhanger ending | Variable | Short punchy followed by blank line |

### Transition Markers

| Marker | Use Case | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| `***` | Major scene break (between arcs) | `***` alone on line |
| `—0o0—` | Small scene transition | UH-style transitional beat |
| Time marker | Flashback/shift | "Sáu năm trước...", "Buổi sáng hôm đó..." |
| Season/weather | Atmosphere shift | Reflects emotional tone of scene |

### Show-Don't-Tell Emotional Beats

Express emotion through physical reactions rather than stating feelings directly:

| Emotion | Physical Manifestation |
|---------|------------------------|
| Anger | nghiến răng, nổ gân, mặt đỏ, hét lên |
| Fear | run bần bật, mặt tái mét, đứng không vững |
| Sadness | nước mắt lã chã, cười méo, thở dài |
| Surprise | tròn mắt, há hốc mồm, giật mình |
| Tension | nghiến răng, tay nắm chặt |

### Cliffhanger Patterns

- End at moment before climax (not after)
- Leave a question in reader's mind
- Use short sentences (1-2 words) for maximum impact
- Pattern from BNsr: "Bảy ngày…" (cliffhanger with ellipsis)
- Avoid full resolution at chapter end

### Foreshadowing Integration

- Plant seeds early in timeline with subtle markers
- Pay off with emotional/experiential beats (not informational dumps)
- Record foreshadow moments in Notes column of timeline
- Align payoff chapters with established timeline anchors
