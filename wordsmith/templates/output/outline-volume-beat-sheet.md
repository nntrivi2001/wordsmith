> **Vietnamese Patterns Note**: When writing in Vietnamese, apply the following style conventions:
> - **Pacing**: Short punchy sentences for crisis/action beats; longer descriptive for emotional payoffs
> - **Rising crises**: Escalate costs progressively; use specific quantifiable changes (not vague statements)
> - **Show-don't-tell**: Express low point through physical reactions (run bần bật, nước mắt) rather than "character is sad"
> - **Mid-volume reversal**: Create false victory/defeat that shifts understanding; new insight should feel earned
> - **Cliffhanger endings**: End on tension or question, not resolution; use short punchy sentences
> - **Emotional beats**: Use nghiến răng for anger, run bần bật for fear, nước mắt for sadness
> - **Scene transitions**: `***` for major breaks, `—0o0—` for smaller shifts
