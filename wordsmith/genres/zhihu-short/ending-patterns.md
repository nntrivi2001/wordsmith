# Zhihu Short Story Ending Patterns

> The ending determines whether readers will recommend your story. This document provides commonly used ending patterns for Zhihu short stories.

---

## Vietnamese Writing Patterns

### From Ta Dung Nhin Vo Lam Toang & Sources:
- Dialogue format: "Content" + action tag
- Inner thoughts: third-person narrative without quotes
- Scene breaks: *** major, —0o0— minor
- Pronouns: mày/tao (close), tôi/ngài (formal), hắn (antagonist)
- First-person internal: cậu/mình

### 8 Error Types to Avoid:
1. Units: mét/cm/km/kg (NOT trượng/dặm/tấc/thốn)
2. Vocabulary: context-appropriate
3. Punctuation: — single (NOT ——)
4. Sentence: complete (subject + predicate)
5. Spelling: natural Vietnamese
6. Connectors: và/nhưng/nên/vì/rồi/thì
7. Subject: explicit in descriptions
8. Natural rhythm

### Genre-Specific Patterns:

#### Xuanhuan/Cultivation:
- Use mét/cm/km for distances (NOT trượng)
- Power levels: Trúc Cơ, Kim Đan, Nguyên Anh
- Dragon descriptions from Bien Nien Su Rong style

#### Isekai/Gaming:
- Status card format with "Keng!" notifications
- Combat: short → explanation → short punch
- Level-up pacing with 5% recovery chance

#### Romance/Fire Strand:
- Show-don't-tell emotion markers
- Building slow, conflict before resolution
- Body language over telling emotions

#### Rules-Mystery:
- Clue placement patterns
- Revelation timing
- Suspense building

## 1. Ending Types Overview

| Type | Emotion | Applicable Genre | Reader Reaction |
|------|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| Big twist HE | Surprise | Pain-to-sweet, mystery | "Didn't see that coming!" |
| Open ending | Aftertaste | Literary, mystery | "The more I think, the scarier" |
| Expected HE | Satisfaction | Sweet stories, pleasure stories | "So sweet!" |
| Heart-wrenching BE | Heartbreak | Pure pain stories | "Kill me with this!" |
| Twist BE | Shock | Mystery, dark | "What the f***!" |

## 2. HE Ending Templates

### 1. Big Twist HE
```
Pattern: Entirely painful → Final reversal → Turns out everything was misunderstanding/protection
Examples:
- Thought he didn't love → Actually silently protected for years
- Thought abandoned → Actually to protect her
- Thought scumbag → Actually undercover/amnesia

Writing points:
- Reversal needs sufficient foreshadowing (plant clues in earlier text)
- Revelation method should have impact
- Give readers the feeling of "suddenly understanding"
```

### 2. Revenge Success HE
```
Pattern: Abused → Counterattack → Tears apart enemies → Gains happiness
Examples:
- Scumbag kneels begging to get back together, female lead turns cold and leaves
- Green tea's reputation destroyed, female lead achieves both career and love
- Evil mother-in-law cut off by son

Writing points:
- Revenge should be satisfying
- Enemy's fate should be miserable
- Female lead should have new happiness
```

### 3. Broken Mirror Reuniting HE
```
Pattern: Separation → Reunion → Misunderstanding resolved → Get back together
Examples:
- Meet years later, discover he's been single waiting for her
- Truth revealed, turns out they were forced apart back then
- He proved his change with ten years

Writing points:
- Separation reason should be sufficient
- Reconciliation should have sincerity
- Give readers the feeling of "finally waiting for this"
```

---

## 3. BE Ending Templates

### 1. Regret BE
```
Pattern: Love → Missed → Lost forever
Examples:
- She waited for him her whole life, when he comes she's already gone
- He finally says it, she's already married someone else
- When truth is revealed, she's already passed away from illness

Writing points:
- Missing should have inevitability
- Regret should be deep
- Leave the sigh of "if only back then"
```

### 2. Dark Flip BE
```
Pattern: Hurt → Go dark → Mutual destruction/destroy everything
Examples:
- She finally got revenge, but also lost herself
- He destroyed everyone, including himself
- She smiled and lit that fire

Writing points:
- Going dark should have sufficient reason
- Ending should have impact
- Let readers "heartached yet helpless"
```

### 3. Twist BE
```
Pattern: Thought HE → Last line reverses → Actually BE
Examples:
- "I woke up and found everything was a dream."
- "She didn't know, he had already died three years ago."
- "Surveillance shows, that night there was only her."
```

Writing points:
- Twist should be unexpected
- Earlier text should have hidden foreshadowing
- Let readers "the more they think, the scarier"

## 4. Open Endings

### 1. Blank Space Style
```
Pattern: Story stops at critical moment
Examples:
- "The doorbell rang. She took a deep breath and opened the door—"
- "He looked at those two lines, silent."
- "She picked up the pen, on the divorce agreement—"

Effect: Let readers imagine the ending themselves
```

### 2. Hint Style
```
Pattern: Not explicit, but hints at ending
Examples:
- "A year later, her belly was already very big." (Hints at reconciliation)
- "Fresh white roses appeared at his grave." (Hints she still loved him)
- "She laughed, laughing like a madwoman." (Hints dark flip)

Effect: Let readers "those who know, know"
```

### 3. Double Interpretation Style
```
Pattern: Ending can have two understandings
Examples:
- "She was finally free." (Relief/death?)
- "They never saw each other again." (Moving on/regret?)
- "Everything is over." (HE/BE?)

Effect: Spark discussion
```

## 5. Ending Writing Techniques

### 1. Emotional Ending Method
```
Don't: Directly write "they lived happily ever after"
Instead: Use a detail to show happiness

Example:
"Ten years later, she still leaves a light on when he works overtime."
"His phone wallpaper, never changed."
```

### 2. Echo Opening Method
```
Opening: She signed the divorce agreement
Ending: She tore up that agreement

Opening: He said "I don't love you"
Ending: He said "I love you, from the very beginning"
```

### 3. Golden Line Ending Method
```
End the entire piece with one powerful line

Example:
- "Some people, missed once is a lifetime."
- "She finally learned to love herself."
- "Turns out, he was always there."
```

### 4. Side Story Hint Method
```
After main story ends, use a short side story to hint at continuation

Example:
"【Side Story】Three years later, outside a hospital delivery room—"
"【Post-credits】The last page of his diary—"
```

## 6. Ending Checklist

### HE Check
- [ ] Is the core conflict resolved?
- [ ] Is reader satisfaction given?
- [ ] Is there sufficient sweetness?
- [ ] Does it echo the opening?

### BE Check
- [ ] Is tragedy inevitable?
- [ ] Is emotional release given to readers?
- [ ] Is there room for aftertaste?
- [ ] Is "pain for pain's sake" avoided?

### Open Ending Check
- [ ] Are hints clear enough?
- [ ] Is discussion space left?
- [ ] Is the feeling of "rushed ending" avoided?

## 8. Vietnamese Webnovel Ending Adaptations

Based on STYLE_GUIDE_VN.md patterns from Vietnamese webnovels (Biên niên sử rồng, Useless hero from another world):

### 8.1 Vietnamese Ending Patterns

| Pattern | Vietnamese Term | Example | Source |
|---------|-----------------|---------|--------|
| Cliffhanger ending | Câu bỏ dở | "Bảy ngày…C" | BNsr Ch.1 |
| Emotional twist | Twist bất ngờ | "Là nó. Thanatos." | BNsr Ch.1 |
| Action cut | Hành động dở dang | "Nhưng khi Tanaka ngồi dậy..." | UH Ch.5 |
| Symbolic ending | Ẩn dụ kết thúc | "Có một nụ cười làm tuyết tan." | BNsr Ch.3 |

### 8.2 Vietnamese Sentence Length for Endings

**Short punchy endings (action scenes):**
```
"Ầm!"
"Chết tiệt!"
"Ngọn lửa bùng lên."
```

**Long emotional endings:**
```
"Lâu đài Chyrse một buổi chiều lặng tuyết buồn tẻ lãng mạn..."
"Có một nụ cười làm tuyết tan."
```

### 8.3 Punctuation for Emotional Impact

| Punctuation | Vietnamese Usage | Effect |
|-------------|-----------------|--------|
| `...` | Suy nghĩ kéo dài, cliffhanger | Suspense |
| `—` | Inner thoughts, dialogue | Emphasis |
| `!` | Shouting, action | Intensity |
| `—0o0—` | Scene transition mid-chapter | Mystery |

### 8.4 Ending Checkpoints (Vietnamese Adaptation)

**HE Check:**
- [ ] Core conflict resolved?
- [ ] Emotional satisfaction given?
- [ ] Sweetness element present (if romance)?
- [ ] Echoes opening hook?
- [ ] Uses Vietnamese sentence patterns?

**BE Check:**
- [ ] Tragedy feels inevitable given character choices?
- [ ] Emotional release given?
- [ ] Room for aftertaste left?
- [ ] Avoids "pain for pain's sake"?

**Open Ending Check:**
- [ ] Hints clear enough for Vietnamese readers?
- [ ] Discussion space left?
- [ ] Rushed feeling avoided?
- [ ] Uses appropriate register (tao/mày vs ngài/tôi)?

### 8.5 Key Differences from Chinese Zhihu Endings

| Aspect | Zhihu (Chinese) | Vietnamese Webnovel |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|
| System resolution | Rare | Common (level up, quest complete) |
| Status card closing | None | Possible (show final stats) |
| Dialogue vs narration | More dialogue | More descriptive narration |
| Open ending style | Question-based | Action/sensory-based |
| Emotional release | Direct emotional statement | Show-don't-tell via body language |

### 8.6 Common Vietnamese Cliffhanger Endings

| Type | Vietnamese Example | Effect |
|------|-------------------|--------|
| Number hook | "Bảy ngày…" | Countdown suspense |
| Name reveal | "Là nó. Thanatos." | Identity shock |
| Action interrupted | "Nhưng khi…" | Tension building |
| System call | "Thẻ trạng thái!" | Gaming hook |
| Threat present | "Thằng khốn đó…" | Antagonist presence |
