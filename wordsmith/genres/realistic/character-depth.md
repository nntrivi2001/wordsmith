# Realistic Fiction Character Depth

> Characters in realistic fiction must be "like real people." This document provides methods for shaping three-dimensional characters.

---

## Vietnamese Realistic Fiction Patterns

### Pronoun System (Đại từ xưng hô)
Vietnamese realistic fiction uses natural, conversational pronouns:

| Relationship | Pronouns | Context |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| Close friends | mày/tao | Intimate, casual |
| Strangers/Formal | tôi/bạn | New acquaintances, work |
| Older respect | anh/chị/ông/bà | Elder respect |
| narration | hắn/nó | Third person referring to others |

**Key Pattern**: Realistic Vietnamese characters shift pronouns based on:
- Emotional state (calm vs angry)
- Relationship closeness (stranger vs lover vs enemy)
- Social context (formal meeting vs private moment)

### Colloquial vs Literary Register
| Colloquial | Literary | Meaning |
|------------|----------|---------|
| đi đường | đường đi | Same meaning, different order |
| làm gì | làm chi | What for |
| sao | như thế nào | How |
| vậy | như vậy | In this way |

**Pattern**: Realistic characters use colloquial in dialogue, literary in inner thoughts/narration.

### Dialogue Voice Characteristics
Vietnamese realistic dialogue is:
- Short and direct
- Uses colloquial language
- Minimal complex punctuation

Narration voice is:
- Longer, more descriptive
- Uses literary forms
- Rich in sensory details

### Show Don't Tell - Realistic Emotions
Vietnamese fiction uses specific physical manifestations:

| Emotion | Vietnamese Physical Expression |
|---------|--------------------------------|
| Fear | "run bần bật", "mặt tái mét", "đứng không vững" |
| Anger | "nghiến răng", "nổ gân", "mắt nổ đom đóm" |
| Sadness | "nước mắt lã chã", "cười méo", "thở dài" |
| Surprise | "tròn mắt", "há hốc mồm", "giật mình" |
| Excitement | "hét lên", "cười phá lên", "nhảy lên" |

### Sentence Structure Patterns
```
BNsr (fast-paced action):
"Kiếm chạm kiếm tóe lửa. Jack bị hất văng về phía sau, lại lao lên."
"-Đánh trả đi - anh rít lên."

BNsr (slow-paced emotional):
"Mùa xuân đến và Ardisia sẽ nở - loài hoa dại thân thảo mọc dày đặc..."

Inner monologue (UH style):
"Khoan, mình đã làm được gì trong suốt mấy ngày qua không?"
```

### Emotional Expression in Relationships
```
Jealousy: "mắt lạnh lùng, cười nhếch mép"
Love: "tim đập nhanh, ngực ấm, thiếu không khí"
Conflict: "hắn nghiến răng; tay vẫn giơ kiếm"
```

## Vietnamese Writing Patterns

### From Ta Dung Nhin Vo Lam Toang & Sources:
- Dialogue format: "Content" + action tag
- Inner thoughts: third-person narrative without quotes
- Scene breaks: *** major, —0o0— minor
- Pronouns: mày/tao (close), tôi/ngài (formal), hắn (antagonist)
- First-person internal: cậu/mình

### 8 Error Types to Avoid:
1. Units: mét/cm/km/kg (NOT trượng/dặm/tấc/thốn)
2. Vocabulary: context-appropriate
3. Punctuation: — single (NOT ——)
4. Sentence: complete (subject + predicate)
5. Spelling: natural Vietnamese
6. Connectors: và/nhưng/nên/vì/rồi/thì
7. Subject: explicit in descriptions
8. Natural rhythm

### Genre-Specific Patterns:

#### Xuanhuan/Cultivation:
- Use mét/cm/km for distances (NOT trượng)
- Power levels: Trúc Cơ, Kim Đan, Nguyên Anh
- Dragon descriptions from Bien Nien Su Rong style

#### Isekai/Gaming:
- Status card format with "Keng!" notifications
- Combat: short → explanation → short punch
- Level-up pacing with 5% recovery chance

#### Romance/Fire Strand:
- Show-don't-tell emotion markers
- Building slow, conflict before resolution
- Body language over telling emotions

#### Rules-Mystery:
- Clue placement patterns
- Revelation timing
- Suspense building

## 1. Three Dimensions of Three-Dimensional Characters

| Dimension | Content | Role |
|-----------|---------|------|
| Social dimension | Profession, class, family background | Defines character's external situation |
| Psychological dimension | Personality, desires, fears, trauma | Defines character's internal drive |
| Moral dimension | Values, bottom lines, gray areas | Defines character's choice logic |

---

## 2. Building Social Dimension

### 1. Professional Identity
```
Not just "she is a doctor"
But rather:
- Which department? (Emergency/Surgery/Pediatrics)
- What title? (Resident/Attending/Chief)
- How many years working? (Newcomer/backbone/senior)
- Professional dilemma? (Promotion/doctor-patient/balance)
```

### 2. Economic Status
```
Specific details:
- Monthly income?
- Any mortgage or car loans?
- How much savings?
- Spending habits?
```

### 3. Family Background
```
Family of origin:
- Parents' professions and personalities
- Family atmosphere (warm/cold/controlling)
- Childhood experiences (trauma/happiness)
- Current relationship with parents
```

### 4. Social Relations
```
- Circle of friends (how many/what quality)
- Relationships with colleagues (harmonious/tense)
- Relationships with neighbors (familiar/strange)
- Social habits (introverted/extroverted)
```

---

## 3. Building Psychological Dimension

### 1. Core Desire
```
Everyone has a core desire:
- To be loved/to be recognized/to be respected
- Security/control/freedom
- Success/money/power
- Belonging/existence/meaning
```

### 2. Deep Fear
```
Fear corresponding to desire:
- Desire for love → Fear of being abandoned
- Desire for success → Fear of failure
- Desire for freedom → Fear of being bound
- Desire for security → Fear of losing control
```

### 3. Psychological Trauma
```
Past wounds affect present behavior:
- Neglected as child → People-pleasing personality as adult
- Once betrayed → Difficulty trusting others
- Experienced poverty → Extremely sensitive about money
- Lost a loved one → Fear of close relationships
```

### 4. Defense Mechanisms
```
How characters protect themselves:
- Escape: Don't face problems
- Attack: Preemptive strike
- People-pleasing: Sacrifice self
- Isolation: Cut off emotions
- Rationalization: Make excuses for self

---

## 4. Building Moral Dimension

### 1. Value Ranking
```
When values conflict, what will the character choose?
- Family vs. career
- Love vs. bread
- Principle vs. interest
- Self vs. others
```

### 2. Moral Bottom Lines
```
Things the character will absolutely never do:
- Some people's bottom line is not lying
- Some people's bottom line is not hurting family
- Some people's bottom line is not breaking the law
- Some people have no bottom line
```

### 3. Gray Areas
```
Choices that trouble the character:
- For the child, should they endure a terrible marriage?
- For promotion, should they do something against their conscience?
- For survival, should they abandon principles?
```

---

## 5. Character Arc Design

### 1. Positive Arc
```
From flaw to growth:
- Inferiority → Confidence
- Escape → Facing
- Dependence → Independence
- Closed-off → Open
```

### 2. Negative Arc
```
From normal to corruption:
- Kind → Cold
- Trust → Suspicion
- Enthusiasm → Numbness
- Persistence → Compromise
```

### 3. Flat Arc
```
Persist in self, change the world:
- Character always adheres to a belief
- Through actions influences those around them
- Proves the power of a certain value
```

---

## 6. Character Relationship Network

### 1. Core Relationships
```
Relationships that most deeply affect the character:
- Family of origin (parents/siblings)
- Intimate relationships (partner/ex)
- Important others (mentor/best friend/rival)
```

### 2. Relationship Dynamics
```
Relationships are not static:
- From stranger to intimate
- From trust to betrayal
- From dependence to independence
- From opposition to reconciliation
```

### 3. Relationship Functions
```
Every relationship has a function:
- Mirror: Reflects the character's truth
- Catalyst: Promotes character change
- Contrast: Sets off character's traits
- Test: Tests character's bottom line
```

## 7. Character Detail Checklist

### External Details
```
- Physical features (doesn't need to be perfect)
- Dressing habits (reflects personality/economy)
- Small movements (when nervous/ thinking)
- Way of speaking (catchphrase/speech rate/word choice)
```

### Life Details
```
- Daily schedule
- Food preferences
- Way of relaxing
- Spending habits
```

### Internal Details
```
- Proudest thing
- Most regretted thing
- Most afraid of thing
- Most wanting thing
```

---

## 8. Character Checklist

### Three-Dimensionality Check
- [ ] Does the character have clear desires and fears?
- [ ] Does the character have past trauma or experiences?
- [ ] Does the character have moral gray areas?
- [ ] Will the character make readers both love and hate?

### Realism Check
- [ ] Does the character's behavior match their background?
- [ ] Does the character's choices have sufficient motive?
- [ ] Are the character's emotional reactions realistic?
- [ ] Does the character have growth or change?

### Uniqueness Check
- [ ] Can this character be replaced by others?
- [ ] Does the character have a unique way of speaking?
- [ ] Does the character have iconic traits?
- [ ] Is the character memorable?
