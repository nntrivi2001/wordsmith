# Dog-Blood Melodrama Plot Templates

> This document provides verified dog-blood melodrama plot templates covering serialized, medium, and short formats—ready to apply directly or combine.

> **Vietnamese Patterns Note**: When writing in Vietnamese, apply the following style conventions:
> - **Pronoun system**: Use `tao-mày` between close characters, `tôi-ngài` with strangers, `hắn` third-person male antagonists
> - **Show-don't-tell**: Express emotions through physical reactions (nghiến răng for anger, run bần bật for fear) rather than stating feelings directly
> - **Sentence rhythm**: Short, punchy sentences for action scenes; longer descriptive sentences for emotional beats
> - **Colloquial vs Literary**: Dialogue uses colloquial language (đi đường, làm gì), narration uses literary style (đường đi, làm chi)
> - **Slang integration**: Use vernacular expressions (thằng chó, khốn) for emotional intensity in appropriate contexts
> - **Scene transitions**: Use `***` for major scene breaks, `—0o0—` for smaller shifts
> - **Cliffhanger endings**: End at moment before climax with short punchy sentences (1-2 words)

## I. Serial Template (800k-1.5M characters)

### Template 1: CEO Chases Wife to Cremation Site

**Core Formula**: `Early pain on heroine + misunderstanding + separation + male lead's regret + long chase + HE`

**Chapter Distribution**:
| Phase | Word Share | Core Content |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| Vol 1: Sweet Illusion | 15% | Contract/interests-based marriage, heroine endures, male lead cold |
| Vol 2: Heartbreak Abyss | 25% | White moonlight appears, misunderstanding deepens, heroine's heart dies |
| Vol 3: Breaking Point | 15% | Truth partially revealed, heroine leaves, male lead shocked |
| Vol 4: Chasing Wife Road | 30% | Male lead regrets, heroine grows, repeated tugging |
| Vol 5: Broken Mirror Reborn | 15% | Truth fully revealed, male lead atones, HE |

**Key Plot Points**:
```
Opening hook: Heroine signs contract/signs marriage (shows unequal standing)
First turning point: White moonlight returns, male lead's attitude changes drastically
Heartbreak climax: Heroine miscarriage/serious illness/framed, male lead doesn't trust
Breaking point: Heroine's heart dies, leaves (runs away with baby/disclaims everything/fakes death)
Chase start: Male lead discovers truth, begins regret
Chase climax: Heroine successful in career/new love, male lead begs for reunion
Darkest moment: Old enemies strike back/new crisis, male lead sacrifices life to protect
Finale: Truth fully revealed, male lead completes redemption, HE
```

## V. Template Usage Guide

### Four-Step Application Method

1. **Choose base template**: Select long/medium/short based on expected word count
2. **Fill in character design**: Apply character settings (reference character-archetypes.md)
3. **Adjust nodes**: Add or remove plot nodes as needed
4. **Add unique features**: Include unique settings or reversals

## VI. Common Questions

**Q: Won't templates feel clichéd?**
A: Templates are frameworks; flesh and blood comes from details. The same wife-chase template, with different character designs, lines, and scene moments, yields vastly different results.

**Q: Can multiple templates be mixed?**
A: Yes, but prioritize. Recommend one main template + 1-2 elements for reference, avoid structural confusion.

**Q: Must word allocation be strictly followed?**
A: Can adjust ±5% based on actual situation, but maintain general proportions, avoid head-heavy or dragon-tail endings.
